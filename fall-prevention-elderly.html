Fall Prevention Tips for the Elderly in Greater Vancouver

Posted on August 20, 2025

Falls are a leading concern for seniors, often leading to injuries that impact independence. At Live At Home Care, we’re committed to helping seniors in Greater Vancouver stay safe and mobile at home. Here are five practical tips to prevent falls and maintain a secure living environment.

1. Remove Hazards Around the Home

Eliminating trip hazards is a key step to fall prevention. Consider these actions:

  • Clear clutter: Keep walkways free of rugs, cords, or loose items.
  • Secure rugs: Use non-slip backing or remove loose rugs.
  • Improve flooring: Fix uneven surfaces or add non-slip tape.

Our personal care services can assist with home safety assessments.

2. Improve Lighting Throughout the House

Good lighting reduces the risk of missteps. Enhance visibility with:

  • Bright bulbs: Use higher-wattage bulbs in key areas like hallways and stairs.
  • Nightlights: Install motion-sensor lights for nighttime safety.
  • Clear pathways: Ensure light reaches all walking areas.

Pair this with our nursing services for professional guidance.

3. Use Assistive Devices

Assistive tools can provide stability and confidence. Options include:

  • Canes or walkers: Use devices suited to your mobility needs.
  • Grab bars: Install in bathrooms and near stairs.
  • Non-slip shoes: Wear supportive footwear indoors and out.

Our team can help select and install the right devices through personal care support.

4. Exercise Safely

Regular movement strengthens muscles and balance. Try these safe exercises:

  • Chair exercises: Improve strength with seated workouts.
  • Tai Chi: Enhance balance with gentle movements.
  • Supervised walks: Walk with support from a caregiver.

Our companionship services can encourage safe activity.

5. Schedule Regular Check-Ups

Regular health assessments can identify fall risks. Focus on:

  • Vision checks: Update glasses or address vision issues.
  • Medication review: Consult doctors about side effects affecting balance.
  • Physical exams: Monitor strength and mobility regularly.

Our nursing services support ongoing health monitoring.
